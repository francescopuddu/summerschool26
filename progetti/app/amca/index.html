Test Setup & Conditions

Fan discharge was ducted to a 711 mm diameter outlet test duct fitted with a log-Tchebycheff pitot traverse station (20 points) located per AMCA 210 straight-duct length requirements upstream and downstream of the plane of traverse. Flow was throttled across the operating range using a calibrated outlet damper; static pressure was read as the average of wall taps at the fan outlet flange, referenced to the traverse-plane velocity pressure for total pressure.

05

Noise Levels — AMCA 300

A-weighted sound pressure level was logged at a fixed 1 m reference position at each test point using a calibrated sound level meter, per the general method of AMCA 300-14. Levels are corrected for speed using the fan sound law (sound power varies with the fifth power of speed, ΔLw = 50·log10(Nr/Nt)) since the test ran at a near-constant, near-rated speed.

06

Vibration Analysis — ISO 10816-3 Zones

Broadband RMS velocity was logged at the drive-end (DE) and non-drive-end (NDE) bearing housings in horizontal, vertical and (DE only) axial directions at every test point. Zone A/B (≤ 2.8 mm/s) is newly-commissioned / long-term acceptable running condition; Zone C, up to the alarm limit, is acceptable for restricted, monitored operation; above the alarm limit is Zone D and unsatisfactory for continuous running; above the trip limit the machine is considered unsafe to operate.

Vibration Spectrum (FFT) Analysis

Each vibration reading was spectrally decomposed into its dominant frequency components: 1× running speed (unbalance signature), 2× running speed (misalignment signature), blade-pass frequency (BPF = running speed × 10 blades — normal aerodynamic tone), its 2× harmonic, and residual broadband/sub-synchronous energy (turbulence, looseness, or bearing-related). 08

Probable-Cause Diagnosis

Every candidate failure mode is scored and ranked using the pass/fail verdict from §03 Results Summary as the primary weight — a failed acceptance criterion scores highest, a warning scores moderately, and a passed criterion contributes little or nothing. Within the vibration-related candidates, the FFT spectrum shape (§07) further identifies which specific fault signature best fits. This produces a single ranked checklist for the operator to work through.

Rule-based screening from acceptance-criteria results and vibration spectrum shape — a diagnostic aid for the operator to verify in the field, not a substitute for inspection.
